How to Track Inventory Without Losing Money

Poor inventory management is one of the top reasons small businesses lose money without realising it. Here's how to do it right.

Inventory is the heartbeat of most small businesses in Cameroon. Whether you run a boutique, a pharmacy, a general store, or a food business — your stock is your money. When inventory is managed poorly, money leaks out in ways that are hard to see until it's too late.

The most common inventory mistakes

1. Not knowing your real stock value

Most business owners know roughly what they have. But "roughly" means you don't know your real assets. If you walked into a bank today, could you produce a document showing the exact value of your inventory at cost price and retail price? With a proper system, this report is generated automatically.

2. Running out of stock without warning

You've lost a sale because an item was finished and you didn't know. It happens to every business that doesn't have low stock alerts. Set a minimum quantity per item, and get a WhatsApp notification before you run out — not after.

3. Staff selling at the wrong price

Without a system, there's nothing stopping staff from selling an item below your intended price — whether by mistake or on purpose. ShopTrack lets you set a minimum price per item. If a staff member tries to sell below it, the sale is blocked and you get an alert immediately.

4. Cost prices visible to staff

Your cost price is your margin. If staff can see what you paid for an item, they can calculate your profit — and that information has no business being visible to them. ShopTrack hides cost prices from all staff accounts. Only the owner sees margin data.

5. No link between purchases and stock

When you receive goods from a supplier, your stock should update automatically. When you make a sale, stock should decrease automatically. In a notebook system, these are manual steps that get skipped. In ShopTrack, they happen automatically with every transaction.

Building a stock management habit

Good inventory management isn't complicated — it's consistent. Record every purchase when goods arrive. Every sale updates stock. Run a stock count once a month and reconcile. ShopTrack makes all of this simple enough to do from your phone, even offline.
