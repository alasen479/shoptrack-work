How to Get a Bank Loan for Your Small Business in Cameroon

Banks don't say no because your business isn't profitable. They say no because you can't prove it. Here's exactly what you need.

Every week, small business owners in Cameroon walk into banks and walk out empty-handed. Not because their businesses aren't profitable — many of them are doing well. They're turned down because they can't produce the documents that banks require.

This guide breaks down exactly what Cameroonian banks ask for, and how to build that documentation starting today.

What banks actually want to see

When a bank evaluates a small business loan application, they're trying to answer one question: can this person pay us back? To answer that, they need evidence of:

  • Consistent monthly revenue
  • Profitability after expenses
  • Business assets (inventory, equipment)
  • Payment history with suppliers
  • A customer base that keeps coming back

The documents they'll ask for

In practice, most Cameroonian banks will ask for some combination of these:

  • Profit & Loss statement — showing revenue, cost of goods, and net profit over 6–12 months
  • Cash flow history — showing money in and money out over time
  • Inventory valuation — a list of your stock and its value
  • Expense records — categorised spending (rent, staff, utilities, etc.)
  • Customer list — evidence of a real, active customer base

The problem most small businesses face

If you're managing your business with a notebook or basic spreadsheet, none of these documents exist in a format that a bank will accept. And you can't go back and reconstruct 12 months of history.

This is why starting early matters. Every day you operate without a proper system is a day without financial records.
