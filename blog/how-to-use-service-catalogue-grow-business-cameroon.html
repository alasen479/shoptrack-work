How to Use a Service Catalogue to Grow Your Business in Cameroon

A service catalogue is one of the most underused growth tools for small businesses in Cameroon. Here is how to create one and use it to convert more customers every day.

When a potential customer messages you on WhatsApp asking what services you offer, what happens next determines whether they become a customer or move on to someone else. If you respond with a typed list 10 minutes later, they've already lost interest. If you respond in 10 seconds with a professional PDF showing your services, prices, photos, and contact details — you look like a real business, and they book.

That difference — between a typed response and a professional catalogue — is the difference between a business that converts enquiries into sales and one that lets them slip away.

What a service catalogue is

A service catalogue is a document — digital or printed — that lists every service or product you offer, with a clear description, price, and ideally a photo. For a salon, it shows every hairstyle, treatment, and price. For a photographer, it shows every package. For a boutique, it shows every category of clothing with price ranges. For a repair shop, it lists every type of repair with estimated costs.

The catalogue serves as your sales tool, your price list, and your credibility signal all in one document.

Why most businesses in Cameroon don't have one

The honest reason most small businesses don't have a service catalogue is that creating one seems time-consuming and technical. You need to list everything, take photos, format it, print it or send it digitally. When you're running a busy shop, this feels like a project that can wait.

The cost of waiting is invisible but real. Every customer who messages you, gets a slow or messy response, and goes elsewhere is a lost sale that never appeared in your records — you never knew it could have been yours.

Where to share your service catalogue

WhatsApp — the most important channel

Every time a customer or potential customer asks "what do you offer?" or "how much is…?" — respond with your catalogue link or PDF. This works better than a typed response in every measurable way: it's faster, it looks more professional, it covers more ground, and it stays in the customer's chat history so they can refer back to it.

WhatsApp Status

Post your catalogue or specific pages from it as your WhatsApp status. Every contact who sees your status is a potential customer who now knows exactly what you offer and at what price. Do this when you have new services, seasonal promotions, or changes to your menu.

Instagram and Facebook

Share individual service cards or pages as posts. A single image showing three services with clear prices is one of the highest-performing content formats for service businesses on social media in Cameroon. It answers the most common question customers have — "how much does it cost?" — before they even ask.

The price transparency advantage

There is a widespread reluctance among Cameroonian service businesses to publish prices. The concern is that competitors will see them or that customers will negotiate harder. In practice, the opposite happens. Businesses that publish clear prices attract more customers — because customers feel respected and informed — and experience less price negotiation, not more. When a price is published and visible, it is perceived as fixed. When a price has to be asked for, it is perceived as negotiable.

Using the catalogue to upsell

A well-designed catalogue does more than answer "what do you offer?" It shows customers what else exists that they didn't know they wanted. A client who comes in for a haircut sees the deep conditioning treatment in the catalogue and adds it. A customer buying a dress sees the matching accessories page and adds two items. This kind of spontaneous upsell — driven by visibility rather than pressure — is one of the most reliable ways to increase average transaction value without spending more on marketing.
