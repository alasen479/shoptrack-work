How to Manage Staff and Protect Your Business From Theft

Most small business theft in Cameroon doesn't happen from break-ins. It happens quietly — through unrecorded sales, below-price selling, and stock that disappears without explanation.

When business owners in Cameroon talk about theft, they usually think about someone breaking in at night. But the reality is that most small business losses happen during opening hours — through unrecorded sales, goods sold below the minimum price, stock that vanishes without explanation, and money that never makes it into the till.

This isn't always deliberate. Some staff genuinely lose track. Some give discounts to friends without thinking. But some know exactly what they're doing — and they rely on you not having a system to catch it.

The four most common ways small businesses lose money to staff

1. Unrecorded sales

A customer pays. The staff member pockets the cash and doesn't record the sale. Without a system that requires every transaction to be entered, this is almost impossible to detect. You might notice at the end of the day that the stock has moved but the money doesn't match — but by then the staff member will have a dozen explanations ready.

2. Below-price selling

Staff sell items to friends or family at a price below what you've set. Sometimes they split the difference with the buyer. If you don't know what everything should be selling for — and you can't see each transaction in detail — this goes unnoticed indefinitely.

3. Stock shrinkage

Items disappear slowly. A dress here, a pair of shoes there. If you count inventory once a month, you'll only notice when the numbers are far off. By then, it's nearly impossible to know when or how it happened.

4. Expense manipulation

Staff report inflated expenses or create fictitious ones. "The delivery cost 5,000 XAF." "We needed supplies — here's the receipt." Without a category-level expense tracking system, these claims are hard to verify.

How to protect your business with a system

Set a minimum price per item — and enforce it automatically

In ShopTrack, every product has a minimum selling price that you set. If a staff member tries to sell below it, they are blocked instantly — the system won't allow the sale to go through. You receive a WhatsApp alert immediately, wherever you are. Not a report at the end of the day. An alert at the moment it happens.

Require every sale to be recorded

When every sale must go through ShopTrack before a receipt is generated, unrecorded transactions become much harder. Customers increasingly expect a receipt, which creates natural accountability. If a customer asks for a receipt and the staff member can't produce one, it raises questions.

Use the full audit trail

ShopTrack logs every action with a staff member's name, the date, and the time. Every sale. Every edit. Every deletion. If a sale was recorded and then deleted — you can see it. If a price was changed — you can see it. Nothing is hidden. This alone changes staff behaviour significantly, because people act differently when they know their actions are being recorded.

Get WhatsApp alerts on every sale

When you receive a WhatsApp notification the moment any sale is made, you have a real-time picture of what's happening in your shop — even when you're not there. If your shop should be busy but no sale notifications are coming through, that's a signal worth investigating.

Do regular stock checks against the system

ShopTrack always shows you the expected stock level for every item. A quick physical count once a week — comparing what's on the shelf to what the system says should be there — takes fifteen minutes and catches discrepancies before they become large losses.

The conversation you don't want to have

Many business owners avoid confronting staff theft because they don't have evidence. An audit trail changes this. When you can show a staff member the exact record of what was deleted, when it was deleted, and who deleted it — the conversation is no longer based on suspicion. It's based on data.

This also changes who you hire and how staff behave from day one. When every new staff member knows that every action is logged, the baseline expectation shifts. Most people, given a system with no accountability, will cut corners. Given a system where everything is recorded, most people won't.
