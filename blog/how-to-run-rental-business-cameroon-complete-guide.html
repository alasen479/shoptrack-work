How to Run a Rental Business in Cameroon — A Complete Guide

Rental businesses in Cameroon are growing fast — equipment, furniture, event items, clothing, electronics. But without the right systems, rentals become a cash flow nightmare. Here is how to run one properly.

Rental businesses are one of the fastest-growing SME categories in Cameroon. Tents and chairs for events. Generator sets for areas with irregular power. Sound systems and lighting for celebrations. Dresses and traditional attire for ceremonies. Baby equipment for young families. Electronics for short-term use.

The demand is clear and consistent. But most rental businesses in Cameroon operate without a proper system — which means lost items, forgotten return dates, unpaid deposits, and disputes that damage both relationships and reputation.

The unique challenges of a rental business

A retail business is relatively simple: you sell something, you receive payment, the transaction is complete. A rental business is fundamentally more complex because the transaction is never complete until the item comes back. This creates four problems that most rental businesses in Cameroon struggle with:

  • Tracking what's out and what's in — at any moment, do you know exactly which items are rented, to whom, and when they're due back?
  • Managing deposits — are deposits being recorded per item, linked to the right customer, and refunded correctly on return?
  • Following up on overdue returns — do customers know when their items are due? Do they get a reminder the day before?
  • Calculating late fees — when an item comes back late, is the additional charge calculated correctly and consistently?

Setting up your rental catalogue

Before you can rent anything out efficiently, you need a clear catalogue — every item you own, its rental price per day or per event, its deposit amount, and its current condition. This catalogue serves two purposes: it tells customers what's available, and it tells you what you own and what state it's in.

In ShopTrack, every rentable item is added to your inventory with a rental price, deposit amount, and availability status. When an item goes out, its status updates automatically. When it comes back, the status resets. You always know what's available without having to check physically.

Creating a rental agreement

A rental agreement is not optional — it is the document that protects you when something goes wrong. It should clearly state: the item being rented, the rental period, the deposit paid, the daily rate, the late fee calculation, the condition of the item at departure, and what happens if the item is damaged or not returned.

ShopTrack generates a rental agreement automatically when you record a new rental. The agreement is sent to the customer's WhatsApp — they receive a professional document before they even leave your premises. This alone eliminates most disputes, because everything is in writing from the start.

Deposits — the most important part of a rental

A deposit serves two functions: it covers you if the item is damaged or not returned, and it creates a financial incentive for the customer to return the item on time. The deposit amount should reflect the replacement cost of the item, not just a token amount.

Every deposit must be recorded against the specific rental, linked to the specific customer, and tracked until it is either refunded on clean return or applied against damage or late fees. In ShopTrack, deposits are recorded per rental and automatically reconciled when the return is processed.

Return reminders — the system that eliminates late returns

The single most effective way to reduce late returns is a WhatsApp reminder sent the day before the item is due. Most customers who return items late do so not because they intended to keep them longer, but because they forgot the exact return date. A reminder eliminates this.

ShopTrack sends an automatic WhatsApp reminder to the customer the day before their rental is due. The message includes the item name, the original rental date, the return date, and your contact details. Customers who receive this reminder return items on time at significantly higher rates than those who don't.

Processing a return

When a rental item comes back, you need to do three things: confirm the item's condition, process any applicable late fees, and refund or retain the deposit accordingly. In ShopTrack, the return is processed in one tap — you confirm receipt of the item, the system calculates any late fees automatically based on the number of days overdue, and the deposit is reconciled. The customer receives a final WhatsApp receipt showing the deposit refunded or the charges applied.

Monthly rental business reporting

A well-run rental business needs to track three financial metrics: total rental income per month, outstanding deposits, and item utilisation rate — what percentage of your inventory is being rented out on average. ShopTrack's reports give you all three automatically, updated in real time. This data tells you which items are your best performers, which need marketing, and whether you should expand your catalogue.
